EDITOR'S SUMMARY

Bottom line

Liv Pure uses a liver-support story to market a weight-management supplement. ClickBank has continued to feature LivPure among notable supplement offers, which supports its commercial legitimacy. The medical framing needs caution: the liver already performs detoxification, and a supplement should not be presented as cleansing the liver, treating fatty liver disease, or guaranteeing weight loss.

Ingredient and evidence overview

Milk thistle / silymarinCommonly marketed for liver support; evidence varies by condition and product.
Green-tea compoundsMay have modest metabolic effects but concentrated extracts have been associated with liver injury in rare cases.
Berberine-related botanicalsMay affect glucose or lipid metabolism and can interact with medicines.
Choline and nutrientsSupport normal metabolism when intake is inadequate; they do not independently cause fat loss.

Safety and interactions

Anyone with diagnosed liver disease, abnormal liver tests, gallbladder disease, diabetes, pregnancy, breastfeeding, or prescription medicines should speak with a clinician before using botanical liver or weight-loss supplements.
